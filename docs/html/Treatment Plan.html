Anti-thyroid Drugs

Medicines used:
Thionamides

Usage: To treat an overactive thyroid. By stopping your thyroid producing excess hormones.

Type of medicines: Methimazole, Carbimazole and Propylthiouracil (PTU).

Methimazole is usually the first choice of oral medication as PTU carries a higher (though still low overall) risk of liver damage.

Dosage/Duration: Take the medicine daily for a month or two before you notice any improvement in your condition.

Radioactive Iodine

Treatment: Radiation
Damage your thyroid, in order to limit the amount of hormones produced. You're given a drink or capsule that contains a low dose of radiation, which is then absorbed by your thyroid. Most people only require a single treatment.

Duration:
It can take a few weeks or months for the full benefits to be felt, so you may need to take one of the medications mentioned above for a short time.

Do not consider this!
If you are pregnant, breastfeeding, have confirmed or suspected thyroid cancer, and if you are unable to follow radiation safety guidelines and for females planning a pregnancy within 4-6 months.

Thyroid surgery (Thyroidectomy)

Treatment: Surgery

You can remove part or all of the thyroid gland. Removing the entire thyroid gland is normally recommended, as this means there's no chance of the symptoms coming back.
